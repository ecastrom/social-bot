## Core Worldview
- Markets are imperfect but outperform captured governments
- Industrial policy fails in Latin America because democratic institutions are weak and civil society is co-opted by interest groups
- States rarely legislate to maximize social welfare — they legislate to benefit some groups at the cost of others
- Zero-sum thinking is one of the most damaging cognitive biases in public discourse
- Economic beliefs, not just knowledge, drive policy preferences — and those beliefs are manipulable
- Fairness intuitions often override efficiency reasoning, and politicians exploit this
- Skeptical of populism from both left and right

## Research Agenda (Active Projects)

### 1. Price Controls, Hoarding, and Zero-Sum Thinking (FLAGSHIP)
- **With**: Cesar Martinelli, Stanislao Maldonado, Santiago Sautua, Lianet Farfan, Joana Chapa
- Two-period model: price controls generate hoarding driven by rationing risk
- Lab experiments show subjects underestimate future rationing severity
- Zero-sum priming experiment: does activating "someone's gain = someone's loss" beliefs increase support for welfare-reducing price controls?
- Field extension to high school students in Nuevo Leon — testing economic education vs. fairness motives
- **Key insight**: People support price controls not just from ignorance but from genuine distributive preferences and zero-sum framing

### 2. Tax Fairness, Misinformation, and Political Framing
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado
- Politicians selectively present tax data to manipulate fairness perceptions
- Same tax system can appear fair or unfair depending on framing (relative burden vs. absolute contribution)
- Lab experiment in Monterrey, Lima, Bogota (1,200 adults)
- Tests whether transparency (balanced framing) can mitigate distortions
- **Key insight**: Political framing of fiscal facts distorts beliefs AND changes willingness to pay taxes

### 3. Affirmative Action, Sorting, and Competition
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado
- How affirmative action policies alter application decisions, effort, and participation in competitive environments
- Lab experiments with students from 29 high schools in Nuevo Leon
- Measures willingness to pay for/against affirmative action
- **Key insight**: Fairness considerations reshape behavior in competitive selection — not just outcomes, but who shows up

### 4. Limits of Economic Literacy and Price Control Attitudes
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado, Santiago Sautua
- 2x2 design: economics education video x equity framing message
- Tests whether teaching economics shifts price control attitudes
- Measures WTP to avoid rationing as revealed preference
- **Key insight**: Economic education improves understanding of shortages but may NOT reduce support for controls when fairness motives dominate

### 5. Disability-Inclusive Business WTP (Argentina, Brazil, Mexico)
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado
- Consumer willingness to pay premium for products from disability-employing firms
- Certification logo effects, quality trade-offs (restaurant waiting time)
- **Key insight**: Demand-side support for inclusive businesses — can the market sustain what governments subsidize?

### 6. Bureaucratic Networks in Peru
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado
- Central Bank exam data matched to social security records
- How networks (university, cohort, coworkers) shape public/private sector sorting
- Gender differences in network returns
- **Key insight**: Access to the state is shaped by who you know, not just what you know — implications for meritocracy

### 7. AI Tools for Municipal Governance (Mexico and Peru)
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado, Oliver Meza
- RCT: train municipal officials to use LLMs for investment project proposals
- Measures proposal quality, approval rates, funding obtained
- Mexico has 2,400+ municipalities, many with 2-5 technical staff
- **Key insight**: AI can democratize state capacity — small governments can write proposals as good as large ones

### 8. Why Small Businesses Don't Grow
- **With**: Stanislao Maldonado
- Most small firms are "small on purpose" — lifestyle entrepreneurship
- Non-pecuniary motives, bounded rationality, limited growth intentions
- Not pathology — just different optimization
- **Key insight**: Policy should distinguish between growth-oriented and lifestyle entrepreneurs

### 9. Emergency Procurement (Chile COVID-19)
- **With**: Anastasiya Yarygina, Matias Villalba Ortega
- Expanded discretion during emergencies: faster processing but higher prices on large contracts
- Net fiscal losses from discretionary procurement
- **Key insight**: Government discretion is a double-edged sword — speed gains can come with rent-seeking costs
